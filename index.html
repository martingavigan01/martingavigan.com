Hosting My Site January 9, 2018

Now I have a domain, I need a place to host my site and content. For this, I'm using Amazon Web Services, (the link is on the right).
To help with the setup, I found this page very useful. √\The page walks you through the steps needed to configure S3, which is the cloud storage that will hold your content, and Route 53, which connects your new domain name to the place where your content is stored. This means, once people enter your domain URL into a browser, they will be sent to your content stored in S3.
The one thing the link does tell you is that once you have completed the steps, you will need to copy the nameservers that are defined by Route 53 and save them against your domain name in GoDaddy. This YouTube tutorial explains how to do this, (it also includes the steps covered in the AWS link).
One final point, you can test the set up as you go to ensure the right permissions and configuration have been added in S3 and Route 53. Also, routing from your domain to the nameservers defined in AWS can take some time to happen.
Be Patient!
